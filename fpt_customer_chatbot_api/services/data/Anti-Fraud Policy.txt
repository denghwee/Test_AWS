## ANTI-FRAUD POLICY

FPT Software Co., Ltd and its subsidiaries (hereinafter referred to as "FPT Software") have issued the "Code of Business Conduct" with the aim of promoting ethical and honest business practices. Preventing fraud is identified as one of the key objectives of this policy.

By establishing detailed provisions on fraud prevention in each country, we aim to enhance our ability to conduct global business activities while earning the trust of the community.

## 1. Objective

FPT Software aims to establish consistent principles and clearly define specific responsibilities for implementing fraud control measures and conducting audits.

## 2. Scope of Application

- · This policy applies to all employees, shareholders/members, consultants, suppliers, contractors, and other third parties directly or indirectly involved in business or collaboration with FPT Software.
- · Any necessary investigation activities (if applicable) will be conducted without considering factors such as length of service, position/title, or relationship with FPT Software for individuals suspected of engaging in fraudulent activities (if any).

## 3. Actions Considered Fraud

- · Any acts of dishonesty or illegal activities with the intent to deceive.
- · Unauthorized use of funds, securities, supplies, or other assets.
- · Violations in the handling or reporting of money or financial transactions.
- · Profiting from insider knowledge of the company's activities.
- · Disclosure of confidential and proprietary information to unauthorized external parties.
- · Disclosure of the company's undisclosed securities activities for the purpose of buying, selling, or seeking profits through the company's securities.
- · Destruction, removal, forgery, or unauthorized use of records, furniture, fixtures, and equipment.
- · Any similar or related irregularities that are known or believed to be illegal or result in unlawful benefits.

## 4. Education and Training

Comprehensive education, training, and awareness programs will be provided to ensure understanding and compliance with fraud prevention regulations.

## 5. Monitoring and Regular Assessments

Regularly conduct audits and risk assessments to review internal regulations, internal controls, and this policy from a competition law compliance perspective. Implement necessary improvements based on audit findings.

## 6. Handling Violations

- · Establish a Compliance Council to promptly and appropriately address any indications or violations of competition law requirements, including this policy, in accordance with the regulations of each country, region, internal rules, and third-party agreements.
- · Violations of competition law requirements or this policy will be handled promptly and appropriately in accordance with internal regulations and/or administrative or criminal sanctions under applicable laws.
